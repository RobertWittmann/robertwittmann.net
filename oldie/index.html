Eye Tracking Immersion (2020)

For my Master thesis, we created a game to test the effect eye-tracking had on immersion and engagement in an emergent narrative. The game featured a procedurally generated world where objects of different biomes would spawn as the player moved around. Objects could be activated to change which biomes the generated objects would belong to. Two identical versions of the games were created: one with the mouse as the main way to interact with the world, and one where eye-tracking was utilised. As the player spawned objects there was a chance of spawning golden objects which were used as a hidden goal of the game as well as dark objects which hindered progress. The entire world was also desaturated, but became saturated wherever you looked, to further encourage exploration and... well looking.

Keywords: Master Thesis | Eye-Tracking | Immersion | Emmergent Narrative | Unity

Musical Glove (2017)

"I feel like Link with the f***ing ocarina." - test participant.
The musical glove project revolved around creating a multimodal peripheral device which would output MIDI notes. By moving the right glove up and down the pitch would increase/decrease, by flexing the right index finger a note would play, and by rotating the left glove the shape of the soundwaves would change. To test the usability of the gloves a Guitar Hero style game was developed. Though there were quite a few kinks to eliminate with accuracy and response time, the general consesus by the testees was that the product was a joy to play with. It was also a blast making it.

Keywords: B.Sc 4th Sem | Audio Processing | Peripheral device

Physical VR Immersion (2017)

My first VR project. The purpose of this project was to test whether adding physical objects, corresponding to those seen in the virtual environment, in the play area would increase immersion in participants. Physical objects were made to be used, in a VR escape room style game, to solve puzzles. To be able to reuse the physical objects twice (as space was rather limited) it was accompanied with an overlapping architecture which meant the users experienced a larger area than the one available (they walked in circles but thought they walked in a straight line).

Keywords: B.Sc 5th Sem | VR | Immersion | Unity

Code-Teaching Table (2018)

My bachellor project was to create a table which would aid in teaching children the fundamental problem solving used for coding. A table was constructed with an opaque glass surface which served as the interaction area. Underneath the table a short-throw projector lit the glass and next to it was a camera which captured the user-interaction. The project featured a puzzle game in which the user would guide a robot to the goal by issuing commands. These commands consisted of a string og blocks which would physically be placed on the table (what the camera was capturing). To solve the puzzles, the user had to use similar problem solving skills as those used for programming.

Keywords: B.Sc 6th Sem | Image processing | Teaching | Unity

VR Fear Avoidance (2018)

When people experience chronic back back, a common cause is often fear avoidance, where the patient neglects movement in fear of pain. As a way to help with the physical therapy needed, we developed a VR game with movement scaling. The scaling would result in the user moving further than they thought, thereby past their fear (so if they moved 90° it might look as if they only moved 60° thereby pushing them further). Alongside the scaling VR a game was made to encourage the movement. The user was asked to pick up pieces of fruit, rotate, and then drop them, scoring more points the further they rotated to drop.

Keywords: M.Sc 1st Sem | VR | Therapy | Unity

Interactive Narratives (2019)

In the 3rd semester of my Masters, I went to Auckland University of Technology in a study abroad programme. One of the courses I took here was "Game Narrative and Interactive Storytelling" in which two of the main assignments, which decided my final grade, consisted of designing and writing two interactive narratives. One in Twine and one in Ink. The first narrative I made was a cyberpunk story where my focus was on delivering the sense of a complete world to the reader, called Cyberdunk (cause I quickly had to come up with a title while sleep deprived). The other narrative, called Internet of Me, revolved around putting the reader in a mystic scenario with only indirect interaction with the world. Both projects beyond their original scope, and both were a blast to work on.
Disclaimer: If you want to avoid spoilers, please play the narratives before reading any of the documentation.

Keywords: M.Sc 3rd Sem | Abroad | Games | Narrative

Hallowgods (2018)

During my bachellor I took a course in game design, in which we had to create a prototype with an iterative design approach. We treated it more like a gamejam and picked our topic for the game at random. The themes wound up being "Halloween" and "Old Gods" (therefrom stems the slightly uninspired title). The setting of the game was that gods had descended from their realms into ours on halloween and went trick-or-treating. With your limited amount of candy you have to make sure your "offerings" go to the right trick-or-treaters. By asking a limited amount of questions to each guest, you have to decipher who's a god, and who's a regular kid, and thereby chose whom to give candy to. Chose wrong, run out of candy, and feel their wrath.

Keywords: B.Sc 6th | Game | Puzzle | Unity
